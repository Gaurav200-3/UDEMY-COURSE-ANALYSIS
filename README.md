# 1. Top-Level KPIs (Key Metrics)

At the top, the dashboard summarizes the most important figures:

Total Courses: 9,760
Total Sales: 23,962.45 (≈ $23.96K)
Total Content Duration: ~198K hours

These are headline metrics giving a quick overview of platform scale and performance.

# 2. Course Distribution Insights
🟣 Paid vs Free Courses
Donut chart shows:
~77.5% Paid courses
~22.5% Free courses
Insight: The platform is heavily monetized with paid content dominating.
📊 Courses by Subject

Bar charts compare categories like:

Web Development (dominant)
Business Finance
Graphic Design
Musical Instruments

👉 Web Development clearly leads in number of courses and activity.

# 3. Subscriber Analytics
🟠 Total Subscribers
Total: ~9 million subscribers
📊 Subscribers by Subject
Web Development → highest (millions)
Graphic Design & Business Finance → mid-range
Musical Instruments → lowest
📊 Subscribers by Level
Beginner, Intermediate, Expert:
Intermediate and Expert slightly outperform Beginner

👉 Insight: Users are not just beginners—there’s strong engagement in advanced content.

# 4. Sales Analysis
🟣 Total Sales
Displayed again as $23.96K
📊 Sales by Subject
Web Development generates the highest revenue (~18K)
Business Finance, Graphic Design, Musical Instruments follow
📊 Sales by Level
Intermediate and Expert courses generate more revenue than Beginner

👉 Insight:

Advanced courses are more profitable
Web Development is the biggest revenue driver

# 5. Content Duration Analysis
🟡 Total Duration
198.05K hours of content
📊 Duration by Subject
Web Development → largest share (~92K hours)
Others significantly smaller
📊 Duration by Level
Fairly evenly distributed across Beginner, Intermediate, Expert

👉 Insight:

Web Development not only leads in sales but also in content volume.

# 6. Trends & Relationships (Right-side charts)

These combo charts (bars + line graphs) show relationships like:

Subscribers vs Courses
Sales vs Subscribers
Content Duration vs Subscribers
Key patterns:
As course count increases → subscribers increase
Sales trend shows gradual decline in some segments
Content duration vs subscribers shows diminishing returns in some areas

# 7. Keyword Analysis

Top keywords driving course content:

"web" → 10,578
"learn" → 9,512
"trading" → 8,979
"beginners" → 7,749
Total keywords: 36,818

👉 Insight:

“Web” and beginner-focused learning dominate search/content strategy.

# 8. Filters / Slicers (Left Panel)

Users can interactively filter data by:

📅 Year (2019–2022)
📚 Subject
🎯 Course Level (Beginner / Intermediate / Expert)

# 9. Detailed Table (Bottom Right)

Shows granular data like:

Course subject (mostly Web Development)
Level (mostly Beginner)
Content duration
Price
Subscribers

👉 Insight:

Many beginner web dev courses with varying duration and pricing.

# 10. Overall Key Insights
🚀 Growth Drivers
Web Development dominates:
Most courses
Most subscribers
Most revenue
💰 Monetization
Paid courses are the majority
Intermediate & Expert levels generate higher revenue
👥 User Behavior
Large subscriber base (~9M)
Strong demand beyond beginner level
📉 Potential Concerns
Some declining sales trends
Content saturation in Web Development

# Final Summary

This dashboard provides a complete business intelligence view of an online learning platform, highlighting:

Scale (courses, subscribers, content)
Performance (sales, engagement)
User preferences (subjects, levels)
Strategic insights (what drives growth and revenue)
